  • Next level spaghetti carbonara
  • Time spent: 30 minutes
    • Ingredients
    • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
    • 150g chicken fillet, finely chopped
    • 1 garlic clove, bashed
    • 200g spaghetti or fettuccine
    • 4 egg yolks (2 of them are optional)
    • 50g cheese
    • 100g mushrooms (optional)
    Directions
    1. To start, warm your serving dishes in the oven or microwave.
    2. Heat oil in a shallow pan and fry the chicken fillet, mushrooms (optionally) and garlic for 10 minutes until golden and crisp. Remove garlic and turn off the heat.
    3. Simmer salted water in a large pan and cook pasta until al dente. Whisk egg yolks in a bowl with a pinch of salt.
    4. Use tongs to transfer pasta to the chicken pan with dripping water. Stir with a spoon and add pasta water if it looks dry.
    5. Quickly add beaten yolks and more pasta water, if needed. Add cheese and stir again. Season with pepper and taste for salt.
    6. Transfer to warmed serving dishes, sprinkle with cheese, and top with egg yolks (optional) before serving.

  • Chicken fillet with mushrooms, baked in sour cream and cheese sauce
  • Time spent: 1 hour 20 minutes
    • Ingredients
    • 700 g of chicken fillet
    • 400 g of champignons
    • 300 g of hard cheese
    • 100 g (3-4 tablespoons) of sour cream
    • 2 tablespoons of flour
    • 50 g of butter
    • 30 ml (2 tablespoons) of vegetable oil
    • 3 cloves of garlic
    • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
    • 1 teaspoon of chicken seasoning.
    Directions
    1. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees. Wash the champignons and dry them with a paper towel. Cut the mushrooms into cubes or slices.
    2. Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan and fry the mushrooms over high heat until excess moisture evaporates and they turn slightly brown (5-7 minutes).
    3. While the mushrooms are frying, cut the chicken fillet into plates that are 1.5-2 cm thick and beat each piece through a food film.
    4. Place the fillets in a baking dish in one layer and add 1/3 teaspoon of salt, 1/5 teaspoon of pepper, and seasoning for chicken. Spread the fried mushrooms on top and grate cheese on a coarse grater.
    5. In a clean frying pan, melt butter and add flour. Fry, stirring, for 1-2 minutes on medium heat. Gradually pour 500 ml of hot water into the fried flour, cook over low heat until smooth, stirring.
    6. Add sour cream and mix. Spread one-third of the cheese and cook, stirring, until the cheese melts. Peel the garlic, press it, and add it to the pan. Add a little salt and pepper (1-2 pinch each).
    7. Pour the prepared sauce into the contents of the mold. Bake in a preheated oven for 40 minutes.
    8. Afterward, take out the mold, sprinkle the contents with the remaining cheese, and return to the oven for another 10-15 minutes until the cheese turns brown.

  • Lazy cabbage rolls in the oven
  • Time spent: 1 hour 30 minutes
    • Ingredients
    • 400 g minced meat
    • 400 g white cabbage
    • 0.5 cups rice
    • 1 egg
    • 1 onion
    • 1 carrot
    • 2 cloves garlic
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    • Ground black pepper to taste
    • Salt to taste
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 1 cup of water
    • Sour cream to taste
    • Fresh greens to taste
    Directions
    1. Finely chop the cabbage and pour boiling water for 10 minutes. Flip the cabbage into a colander and let the liquid drain.
    2. Wash the rice and boil over low heat until half cooked, about 10-12 minutes after boiling.
    3. Peel and finely chop the onion. Peel the carrots and grate them on a coarse grater. Peel the garlic and squeeze it out through a press.
    4. Fry the onion and carrot in sunflower oil for 7 minutes over medium heat.
    5. Mix minced meat, cabbage, rice and fried vegetables. Add egg, ground black pepper, crushed garlic and salt. Mix everything well.
    6. Form cutlets from minced meat with wet hands.
    7. Dilute the tomato paste with water. Put the cutlets in a baking dish, pour tomato paste diluted in water (or tomato juice).
    8. Turn on the oven and preheat to 180 °C. Bake lazy cabbage rolls in the oven for one hour.
    9. Serve lazy cabbage rolls with sour cream and fresh herbs. Boiled potatoes or mashed potatoes can be served as a side dish to such cabbage rolls.

  • Lasagna
  • Time spent: 1 hour
    • Ingredients
    • 1 onion
    • 2 Bulgarian pepper
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 500 g minced meat
    • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 2 tablespoons ketchup
    • 200 g cheese
    • 200 g сottage cheese
    • 250 g cream
    • 15 lasagna plates (The plates were narrow)
    Directions
    1. Boil the lasagna plates in salted water for about 10 minutes until they are "al dente" (slightly undercooked).
    2. In a pan, sauté chopped onion and sliced pepper in olive oil. Add minced meat, season with salt and pepper, and cook until crumbly. Stir in tomato paste and ketchup, then simmer for a bit.
    3. Grate hard cheese. Save some for later.
    4. Grease a deep baking dish with 4 tablespoons of cream. Mix the remaining cream with cheese and cottage cheese.
    5. Assemble the lasagna in layers: Start with a layer of plates, followed by 1/3 of the meat mixture, 1/3 of the cheese and cream mixture, and then another layer of plates. Repeat this twice more: meat mixture, cheese and cream mixture, plates. Finish with a layer of meat and top it with grated hard cheese.
    6. Bake in a preheated 180-degree Celsius oven for 40 minutes.

  • Potato accordion with chicken, baked in foil
  • Time spent: 1 hour 10 minutes
    • Ingredients
    • 1 kg potatoes (6 pieces)
    • 250 g Chicken fillets
    • 100-150 g cheese
    • 60 g butter
    • 1 teaspoon dried garlic
    • Tomatoes (optionally)
    • Salt (to taste)
    • Ground black pepper (to taste)
    Directions
    1. Preheat the oven to 200 degrees Celsius.
    2. Peel, wash, and dry the potatoes. Place a potato on a tablespoon or between two skewers and make frequent, deep incisions about 1 cm apart, without cutting through the end.
    3. Sprinkle each potato with garlic, salt, and pepper, ensuring the spices get into the incisions.
    4. Insert thin slices of chicken (also tomatoes if you want) and cheese into each cut in the potato.
    5. Place each prepared potato on a piece of foil.
    6. Add a piece of butter on top of each potato.
    7. Wrap the potatoes in foil, folding the ends to prevent any drips during baking.
    8. Arrange the foil-wrapped accordion potatoes on a baking sheet.
    9. Bake in the preheated oven at 200 degrees Celsius for 40-50 minutes.
    10. Afterward, open the foil, baste the potatoes with the released juices, and return them to the oven for another 7-10 minutes until they brown nicely.
